Instructions for the Skating Simulation

In this simulation, you can observe how a skater moves across frictionless ice in response to the pull of an elastic cord. The skater starts motionless and your job is to exert a force on the skater. You exert that force by touching the skater with the mouse pointer and pressing the mouse button. An elastic cord then appears between the skater and the mouse pointer, and you can stretch that cord by moving the mouse pointer to the right or left. As the cord stretches, the skater experiences a force toward the mouse pointer. The more you stretch the cord, the stronger that force becomes. When you release the mouse button, the cord releases the skater and the skater no longer experiences a force.

To help you control how hard you pull on the skater, you can choose one of three different elastic cord pull strengths: small, medium, and large. The larger the cord’s pull strength, the more force it exerts on the skater as you stretch it.

 You can also adjust the skater’s mass over a range from 20 kg to 100 kg. A child of 20 kg will respond differently to a given force than a large man of 100 kg.

To begin experimenting with the simulation, place the mouse pointer on the skater, press the mouse button, and drag the mouse pointer to the right. You’ll see the elastic cord stretch and the skater begin to move. You’ll also see arrows indicating the force that the elastic cord is exerting on the skater and two of the skater’s quantities of motion: acceleration and velocity. Release the mouse button. The elastic cord will vanish, along with its force, but the skater will continue to move.

You can stretch or release the cord repeatedly by pressing or releasing the mouse button. Notice that to exert a constant force on the skater, you must move the mouse with the skater so that the length of the elastic cord doesn’t change. See if you can make the skater travel back and forth across the ice. Can you bring the moving skater to a stop? How does the skater move if you keep your end of the elastic cord at a fixed point on the ice?

If the skater skates off the ice, you can return the skater to the center by pressing the “Reset skater” button.
